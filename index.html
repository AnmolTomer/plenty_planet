TrES-2b

TrES-2b has been dubbed the ‚Äúdark planet‚Äù . If we were able to view it directly, it would likely just look like a coal-black ball of gas. At 1800¬∞F (1000¬∞C) the planet is way too hot for clouds, which would help reflect the star‚Äôs light. The red tinges are areas of superheated gas. HD 189773b

HD 189773b is relatively close to us, at only 63 lightyears away. It is blue but unlike Earth, however, the weather gets more extreme, because intense pressure and temperature turns silicate particles in the atmosphere into glass. As if that doesn‚Äôt sound dangerous enough, the winds have been estimated to gust at 4,000 mph (7,000 km/h) which really whips those glass particles around.

55 Cancri e

55 Cancri e is twice the size of Earth but is nearly 8 times more massive and twice as dense. Last fall, researchers deduced that the mass of the planet was largely carbon. Due to the pressure and surface temperature of 4892¬∞F (2700¬∞C) it very well could have formed diamond. It is so close to its parent star it takes a mere 18 hours for the planet to complete a full orbit.

PSR B1620-26b

Nicknamed ‚ÄúMethuselah,‚Äù PSR B1620-26b is the oldest known exoplanet. The planetary system formed approximately 12.7 billion years ago, when the Milky Way galaxy was in its infancy. It is located in the Scorpius constellation about 12,400 lightyears away. Methuselah orbits binary stars and goes around them in a circumbinary orbit.

TrES-4

Located 1,400 lightyears away in the Hercules constellation, TrES-4 is the largest exoplanet with over 1.7 times the size of Jupiter, it has an extremely low density and is categorized as a ‚Äúpuffy‚Äù planet.
